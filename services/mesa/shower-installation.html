Shower Installation in Mesa, AZ

A great shower is built in the wall, not picked off a shelf — the valve and drain work decide everything.

Whether it's a new bathroom, a tub-to-shower conversion, or an upgrade to a rain head and body sprays, we handle the plumbing that makes it work: correctly set valves, properly sloped and sealed drains, pressure-balanced or thermostatic mixing, and supply lines sized so the 'spa shower' actually feels like one.

Conversions are our most-requested shower project in Mesa — and they live or die on drain relocation and waterproofing prep. We coordinate cleanly with your tile installer or contractor so the plumbing lands exactly where the finished design needs it.

Shower Installation Questions, Answered

Can you convert my bathtub to a walk-in shower?

Yes — it's one of the most popular bathroom upgrades, especially for accessibility. The plumbing work involves relocating the drain and setting a new valve; we'll flag anything about your slab or framing that affects cost before demo.

What's a thermostatic valve and is it worth it?

It holds your set temperature regardless of what the rest of the house is doing — no cold shock when a toilet flushes. For multi-head or high-end showers it's absolutely worth it; we'll quote both options.

My current shower has weak pressure. Can installation fix that?

Usually yes — undersized supply lines, a restrictive old valve, or scale buildup are the common culprits. Fixing it during installation is far cheaper than after the tile is up, which is why we size lines for the heads you actually want.
